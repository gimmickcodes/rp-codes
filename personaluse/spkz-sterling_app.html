History

Your mother was a rising star: a lounge singer in the days when stardom is a concept still in adolescence, her career ascending in parallel with the phrase 'celebrity culture'. Endowed with the looks, poise, and voice to make it big, she sought more glamor than countryside origins could provide; finds it in Elysium City in an succession of suitors, the sorts who fall in love at one glance, one song, and who make good on it with favors and flings.


Her popularity—their gifts—whet an appetite for luxury beyond the family's means—certainly beyond her willingness to moderate spendings or admit the fickleness upon which she depends.


In this way do you grow up at wealth's fringes, close enough to finger its trappings, see the spotlight gleaming off brooches and silver-gilt words; yet have as home everything the stage is not: close walls with crooked doorframes, shared hallways bleak as any other tenement housing.


By day you're another public school whelp in a system over full. Come evening, you accompany your mother as she tours lounges exclusive to the rich. You sit in bar corners and backstage of music halls while she gallivants and flirts and fritters away her prizes. More than once you return to the slums alone. This is what passes for supervision beside a starlet's knee, and you spend it with one eye on opulence out of reach even as necessity compels you to start managing her accounts.


It didn't arrive easy, your keenness for numbers. But, perforce, it did come young.


In time, she lands a recurring booking at the — Hotel, where her nightly shows draw nightly crowds. They draw, too, the eye of its hotelier until a rapturous affair, then freak accident, drops the building under her name. That she has no taste for management is no barrier. Enter X Savoy, who snaps it up eagerly in a whirlwind romance meets marriage of convenience.


From rags to riches, gutter to glamor. you'll do your damndest to fit in, but you’ll never lose your striver's sensibilities. Nowadays, while old-blood nobles make rounds through rarefied circles, it's your magazines that bring their customs and their fashions to the common forum, dictating the trends of the day. Raised by an aspirant yourself, you know what they crave: glimpses of wealth, visions for their ambition. You print these into dreams they can moonlight in with the turning of a page. You turn it into gold.
