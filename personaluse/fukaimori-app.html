Divine Gatekeeper
Jikoku's Gate [SUB-SKILL] - He can summon spirits by opening a gate between the material and spiritual planes. In their base form they appear as black, cloud-like entities but can condense into the shape of shadowy animals. Purifying magic can forcefully dispel them, after which there is an 3-post (15-minute) cool down before they can be resummoned.

Interior Gate [SUB-SKILL] - He draws the same energy from the other plane directly into himself. This aura can then be channeled into blows or weapons to enforce them or extend his reach. Lasts 5 posts. Shares a 3-post cool down with Jikoku's Gate — neither one can be used immediately after the other
Pathmaker
Ingress [SUB-SKILL] - Opens a passage through any surface he's touching, rendering a part intangible. Only one pathway can be made at a time, spanning 3 by 3 meters max and reaching a depth of 2 meters. Anything caught halfway when the passage closes will get stuck, and travelers are unable to see or hear while passing through. Doorways resemble a watery patch on the wall, obstruction, etc, because of the loss of solidity, but are otherwise unobtrusive.

Terrestrial Gate [SUB-SKILL] - By visualizing a clear exit point, he can create doorways for traversing up to 10 km. If destination is insufficiently focused, he runs the risk of going "off-course", with the exit appearing somewhere less than ideal — ten stories off the ground, for instance. As such, he'll often mark locations with talismans. Up to one other person can follow him if they're touching him. Can be used once per thread, or twice a day.
Paths of Motion
Divergence [SUB-SKILL] - He can manipulate the directionality and speed of moving bodies and projectiles, including spells, within a 10 meter radius. Can only be applied to one target at time; the larger and more powerful it is, the lower his precision and ease of control.

Flux [SUB-SKILL] - Sacrificing control for scope, he can produce an aura with a radius of 11–40 meters. Projectiles released within this space behave erratically, whereas those fired at it from outside will be made to veer off-course as though subtly repelled. Lasts up to 4 posts (15 minutes). Divergence cannot be used while this is in effect.
Paths of the Spirit
Pathfinder [SUB-SKILL] - He can sense and visualize the reiryoku trails left by all living things. Those with more reiryoku leave behind stronger trails. Similarly, the more familiar he is with a persons' energy, the easier their trail is to follow.

Turnkey [SUB-SKILL] - He can seal a person's ability to access their reiryoku, rendering them temporarily incapable of magic. The effect lasts 3 posts (15 minutes) on those at a lower ascension level; 2 posts on those at an equal level; and 1 post on those higher. Requires physical contact.
Wayfarer's Blessings
Wayfarer's Welcome [SUB-SKILL] - Exerts a certain aura on people that makes them feel oddly compelled to be generous and respond positively to his requests. The effect is strongest with eye contact.

Wayfarer's Rest [SUB-SKILL] - He can give others a small energy buff, reinvigorating them, adding a lightness to their step. While the blessing won't heal them, it can lessen the pain of an injury, assuming it's not overly severe. Last 3 posts.

A woman stands at the castle gates. Dust-cloaked, sandals worn, clothing patched from travel, she knocks, and when the doors open, she presents the babe in her arms, insists he has the lord's eyes. The father comes on a whim, sees it is for true, and turns her away all the same.

act two
Discarded dalliance, she has no one in Oihama and nowhere to return, only that distant border town from whence she'd come and the hatred of a jilted partner. And so she starts anew: a new hole, dug deeper every year as debts pile high.

act three
She writes him, your mother. Because a courtesan's wages aren't enough, because health fades, and even dreams can be filling when you have little else. Frequently, sporadically, pleadingly, she writes — of your resemblance, of your magic, of old promises and other things you've stopped keeping track of, all in hopes he'll claim you son and provide everything she can't. No reply will ever come, only the odd gift like a bone for dog, and somehow that is worse.

act five
At nine, you glimpse your father in the flesh. Mother points him out from the retinue, but where she sees hope, you see privilege, you see eyes that go right through you. Here's the man who left her, and no different than the rest.
第六幕
act six
You're on your own, but you're ok. You've a knack for magic, always have — for getting into places, for winning over marks. You know the myths, the legends, and when friends whisper tennin you don't trouble to correct them, only ask they keep it quiet if they want to get ahead. Yokai are a distant threat when hunger's around the corner, so all the doors you open are ones that lead to jewels.
第七幕
act seven
It must be fate that brings you to Oihama. Fate, that this is where you're caught. This time, this time when he sees you, you see recognition come two-fold: a feather on his bloodline, another weapon in their arsenal. Your mother is less than a footnote in the story they put out, that of a long lost son come home at last. No matter; none are fit to speak her name.
第八幕
act eight
You still marvel at how swift people are to change their tune. A name appended, a dash of divinity, and everyone takes note, is eager for your favor. They bribe you with respect, with their hopes and expectations; they give it all so freely it should come as no surprise when you give nothing in return. After all, you didn't come here just to serve.
