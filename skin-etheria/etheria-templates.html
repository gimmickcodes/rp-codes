The Court of Amethyst

Of monsters, Gilded, and forests

The Amethyst Kingdom was a land rich with forestry and personality before Concordia. Guilds of all kinds existed: for thieves, adventurers, scribes, finesmiths, crafters, and more. The disposition of the average Amethysian was quite cheery and it was typical for many young adults to leave the home and find their own path in life, even if it took them outside the kingdom. Amethyst saw many nomadic groups and immigration throughout the land, mostly because Amethyst was always seen as the kingdom of adventure—but also because it offered natural sights of beauty like no other.

When Concordia occurred, Amethyst’s land suffered the most of the seven courts. A massive fissure split down the middle of the land, cratering almost the entire kingdom. Many lives and structures were lost. By the time the seven courts came together, the only guild left standing was the Adventurer’s Guild who were quite the resilient people. House Drystan partnered with the guild to rebuild the nation as a forested canyon, but when the undeity was captured by Jade, Amethysians discovered soon after that many of them had come down with a unique condition: their blood turned gold and was rich with ether.

They became known as Gilded, and the rate of affliction varied from person to person. Those with blood as gold as the sun suffered from other symptoms such as a weakness to iron and the inability to tell an outright lie. The occasional pointed ears or glowing skin accompanied their condition as well. More frighteningly, they learned that they were perfect prey for the Harrowed of Ruby who needed to feast on ether-rich blood to survive. This volatile history has led to many new guilds and guild-like groups coming together.

The land still retains a lot of its fantastical qualities: monsters, enchanted forests, and even the occasional curse. Its Adventurers Guild remains a source of pride for the court, conducting lots of international travel and hunting—of monsters, treasures, people, flowers, and more. House Drystan and its military partner frequently with the guild, as they often have a lot on their plate between village attacks by monsters and Rubians licking their lips. Within the canyon-laden Court of Amethyst are lush grasslands, forests, and many rebuilt towns. Several pulley systems are employed around the canyons to allow in-and-out travel. Amethyst offers many artful sights worth visiting: bubbling ravines, cascading waterfalls, and plains dotted with asters—all surrounded by enormous rock walls.

Religion

Amethyst brought over its deity, Shina (Divine of Discovery) and has since adapted to Adornism to worship all Four Divines. However, the face of Shina is prevalent all over: from the architecture to the military cloak pins to tree trunk carvings. Because ether is so prevalent in the land, they are firm believers that it is a blessing by the divines. Most Amethystians revere what Shina represents: adventure, exploration, and spontaneity.

Culture

The people of Amethyst are hearty and free-spirited. They enjoy adventure, danger, and freedom. Because most citizens are quite resourceful, poverty is rarely an issue. Many hunt, craft, and barter on their own without the government needing to involve themselves. Deviations from normal bodies are commonplace here as a side effect of being Gilded. Denizens of Amethyst won’t bat an eye at animal ears, claws, glowing skin, cat eyes, and so on. Towns are pretty small, and forest communities are even smaller, creating a strong kinship all around.

Resources

Amethyst is known for its supreme hunting weapons and traps. They also produce special leather used by many militaries in other courts. Typical resources include forest products like fur, lumber, and monster products.

Positions in Amethyst

Led by House Drystan

Amethysian Council

House Drystan has ruled as a council for the past two hundred years. Since the change from monarchy to council was instated per the old monarch’s command, the council has always voted in Drystan members to replace vacant spots, with stalemates deferring to the longest tenured. The council of five votes on all matters, though certain jobs and areas are often taken up by specific Grand Princes or Princesses. House Drystan does not have a “main line” due to the nature of its setup, so all those bearing the Drystan surname are referred to as Princess and Prince. House Drystan, the military, and the Adventurer’s Guild partner frequently on matters. Sample positions in Amethyst include but are not limited to: dukes/duchesses, captains, knights, adventurers, and hunters.

Military

The Amethystian military is on the smaller side, with its Lord Commander at the helm. This unit partners with the Adventurer’s Guild when needed. Ranks include Captains and knights.

Adventurer's Guild

The Adventurer’s Guild is the only guild that survived Concordia, though others have risen since. Its members tackle all sorts of adventures around the seven courts including monster hunting, treasure-seeking, bounty-hunting, exploration, and more. It is led by a single Guild Master.
