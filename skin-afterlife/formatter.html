Premise

One moment you were elsewhere, and the next you’re here: an endless wood. A dark realm, and cold, with wolves, you think, or something like them, and you—alone, with no arms with which to fight nor shelter behind which to hide.

What were you doing? Where were you going? You struggle to remember at first before it all went black, before nothingness. Before your eyes opened here, against still air that tugged at your very essence, on ground that felt not meant to hold you. And whichever way you stumbled, however long you groped, there was nowhere, nothing else—until you found the monastery. Whether this was purgatory or whether this was hell, here, at least, was a refuge.

Inside, no one is surprised by your arrival. Ask, and they confirm your suspicions: you’d died—and so had they. They, like you, trickled in from the woods. They, like you, have been here for days, or decades, or centuries, with the caretaker who greeted you playing deaf to further questions, and with the ever-present worry that this bastion will be breached.

But now the Builder comes along, and he speaks of Beyond: reach it, and who knows? You might find a path back to a world more familiar, a means of turning death to merely near-death. You might find paradise. More likely you’ll die trying, and accelerate your dissolution. So yes, you’re dead. Yes, you’re stuck here. But maybe not for long, and, for better or for worse, definitely not forever.

Welcome to the Monastery of St. Divenire.

Note

After We Die is a statless, OC-only afterlife roleplay. The site begins contained within the confines of a single monastery, but the expansion of the setting via multiversal dungeons and events are a key focus of the plot, with overarching lore for the nature of this realm and a defined ending. Characters can die and respawn. They can be veterans or newly dead. They can amass items and expand their kit. Above all, they can come from any dimension, time period, or previous site. The only requirement is that they’re human, or at the very least appear as such. How much and whether you engage with these elements is up to you, but be forewarned that the state of the monastery itself will change based on events.
